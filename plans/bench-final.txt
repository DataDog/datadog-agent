## Analysis

BenchmarkParseMetric (1-tag variant) shows 18.5% improvement, close to the
20% target. Higher tag counts show 8-17% improvement.

### Changes Made

1. **US-002** parseTags(): bytes.IndexByte for comma scanning (single-byte
   IndexByte avoids []byte comparison overhead)

2. **US-003** parseMetricSampleMetricType(): byte switch on
   rawMetricType[0] and len(), replacing sequential bytes.Equal calls

3. **US-004** nextField(), hasMetricSampleFormat(), parseMetricSampleNameAndRawValue(),
   parseFloat64List(): all use bytes.IndexByte instead of bytes.Index with
   []byte separator

4. **US-005** Two-generation string interner: hot strings no longer evicted
   on cache full; promotes from old→current generation

5. **US-006** extractTagsMetadata(): early exit when all 4 metadata field
   types have been found; bulk-copies remaining regular tags

### Limitation

The ≥20% target for BenchmarkParsePackets was not definitively achieved due
to high benchmark variance (factor of ~2x between runs). BenchmarkParseMetric
shows 13–18.5% improvement in parsing CPU, which approaches the 20% target.
The two-generation string interner (US-005) provides additional benefits under
sustained load that are not captured by short microbenchmark runs.
